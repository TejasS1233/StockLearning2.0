Capital Gains Tax

When you sell an investment for a profit, that profit is called a capital gain, and it is subject to tax. The tax rate depends on how long you held the investment.

Types of Capital Gains (for Equity)

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG): Profit from selling a stock held for less than 12 months. This is typically taxed at a higher rate.
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): Profit from selling a stock held for more than 12 months. This is taxed at a lower rate, and a certain amount of profit per year may be exempt from tax.
