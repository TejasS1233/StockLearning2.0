Investor Psychology

Understand how common emotional biases like fear and greed can impact your decisions and learn to invest with discipline.

Emotional Pitfalls

The biggest enemies of a successful investor are often their own emotions. Fear can cause you to sell at the bottom of a market crash, locking in losses. Greed and FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) can cause you to buy into speculative bubbles at the peak, just before a correction. Recognizing these impulses is the first step to overcoming them.

Common Cognitive Biases

  • Confirmation Bias: The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms one's preexisting beliefs.
  • Herding: The tendency for individuals to mimic the actions (rational or irrational) of a larger group.
  • Overconfidence: The tendency to overestimate one's own ability to predict market outcomes.
