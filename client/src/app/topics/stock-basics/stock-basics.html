üìà Stock Market Basics

Learn the fundamentals of how stock markets operate, essential terms, and what you need to get started on your investment journey.

The share market is where you can buy and sell parts of a company, known as stocks or shares. It's a popular way to invest and grow your wealth over time. The term share market typically refers to trading only in company shares. The broader stock market includes shares but also other financial instruments like bonds, mutual funds, and derivatives.

In India, trading happens on official platforms called stock exchanges. For a stock to be traded, it must be listed on an exchange. The two primary exchanges in India are:

  • Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)
  • National Stock Exchange (NSE)

üìú Types of Share Markets

Primary Market

This is where a company first offers its shares to the public to raise money. This process is called an Initial Public Offering (IPO). Think of it as buying a new product directly from the manufacturer for the first time.

Secondary Market

This is what most people refer to as the "stock market." Here, investors buy and sell stocks from each other at prevailing market prices. The company is not directly involved in these transactions. Prices are set by supply and demand.

üöÄ How to Invest: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Select a Reputed Broker.

    Choose a trading firm registered with the SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India). Your broker provides the platform and tools you need to trade.

  2. Open Demat and Trading Accounts.

    A Demat Account holds your shares in electronic form. A Trading Account is used to place buy and sell orders. These are linked to your bank account.

  3. Add Funds to Your Trading Account.

    Transfer the money you wish to invest from your bank account to your trading account. This will be your starting capital.

  4. Research and Choose Your Stocks.

    Don't invest based on rumors! Look at a company's financial health, its position in the industry, and future growth potential.

  5. Decide on Your Investment Amount.

    Figure out how much you're comfortable investing. It's wise to diversify your investment across different stocks to manage risk.

  6. Place a Purchase Order.

    You can buy a stock at its current market price (a market order) or set a specific price you're willing to pay (a limit order).

  7. Execute the Order.

    Once you confirm your purchase, your broker executes the order. The shares will soon appear in your Demat Account.

  8. Track Your Investments.

    Regularly monitor your portfolio. Keep up with market news and company performance to decide when to hold or sell your stocks.
